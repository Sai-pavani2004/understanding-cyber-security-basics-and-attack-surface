Cybersecurity Basics & Attack Surfaces
üìå Introduction

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks often aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, disrupt services, or extort money from users or organizations.

An attack surface refers to all the possible points where an attacker can try to enter or extract data from a system.

This repository provides a basic understanding of cybersecurity concepts and common attack surfaces to help beginners learn how attacks happen and how they can be prevented.

üîê Cybersecurity Basics
1. Confidentiality

Ensures that information is accessible only to authorized users.

2. Integrity

Ensures data is not altered or tampered with without authorization.

3. Availability

Ensures systems and data are available when needed.

These three principles are known as the CIA Triad.

‚öîÔ∏è What is an Attack Surface?

An attack surface is the total number of vulnerabilities, entry points, or attack vectors that attackers can exploit to gain unauthorized access to a system.

The larger the attack surface, the higher the risk.
